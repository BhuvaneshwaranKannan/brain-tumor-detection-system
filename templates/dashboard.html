Brain Tumor Knowledge

Familiarize yourself with neuro-oncology fundamentals and file specifications.

A brain tumor is an abnormal growth of cells within the brain. Benign tumors are non-cancerous, while malignant tumors are cancerous and can grow rapidly. Early detection significantly improves treatment outcomes.

  • Glioma: Originates in the glial cells that surround and support neurons. Often malignant.
  • Meningioma: Forms on membranes that cover the brain and spinal cord just inside the skull. Typically benign.
  • Pituitary: Develops in the pituitary gland at the base of the brain. Usually benign but affects hormones.

NIfTI (.nii) files are a standard format used in neuroimaging. They contain 3D volume data representing brain slices. The ".gz" extension indicates the file is compressed. You can obtain sample sets from open medical databases like TCIA (The Cancer Imaging Archive) or Kaggle.
